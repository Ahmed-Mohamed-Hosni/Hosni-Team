# Team Social Contract – GCHA Project

## Section 1: Conflict Resolution

If a conflict happens between global sites such as Berlin and Bangalore, the team will follow this 3-step process:

1. Each side explains the issue using facts, not personal criticism.
2. The London PMO reviews the impact on scope, schedule, quality, and risk.
3. A final decision is documented in GitHub and communicated to all affected sites.

This process ensures that conflicts are solved professionally and do not delay the GCHA project.

## Section 2: Communication Norms

The team will use the Golden Window for important meetings between London, Berlin, Cairo, and Bangalore. All team members must respond to important Jira or GitHub updates within 24 hours.

Urgent risks, blockers, or security issues must be reported immediately to the London PMO. This prevents communication delays caused by time-zone differences.

## Section 3: Ethical Commitment

If a manager or stakeholder asks the team to ignore a bug, especially one that affects patient safety or data privacy, the team will reject the request and report the issue formally.

According to the ACM/IEEE PUBLIC principle, the safety and welfare of the public must come before schedule pressure or business convenience.

The GCHA team commits to transparency, honesty, and professional responsibility in all project decisions.
